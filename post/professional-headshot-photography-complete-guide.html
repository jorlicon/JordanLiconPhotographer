Professional Headshot Photography Guide: What Makes a Great Headshot

Published May 5, 2026 | By Jordan Licon Photography | Reading time: 8 minutes

A professional headshot is one of the most important images in your professional brand. Whether you're building a LinkedIn presence, launching a business, or pursuing an acting career, your headshot communicates credibility, approachability, and professionalism before you say a word.

But what exactly makes a professional headshot great? In this guide, we'll break down the elements of effective headshot photography and help you understand what to look for when choosing a headshot photographer.

What is a Professional Headshot?

A professional headshot is a portrait photograph specifically designed for business, career, or professional purposes. Unlike casual photos, professional headshots are strategically composed, professionally lit, and carefully edited to present you in the best possible light while maintaining authenticity.

Professional headshots are used for:

  • LinkedIn profiles - Create a strong first impression in your professional network
  • Corporate websites - Appear credible and approachable on your company's leadership or team pages
  • Marketing materials - Use in brochures, case studies, and promotional content
  • Casting and auditions - Represent your range and personality to casting directors
  • Press and media - Provide journalists with a professional image of you
  • Email signatures - Add visual credibility to your communications

The 5 Elements of a Great Professional Headshot

1. Professional Lighting

Lighting is the foundation of every great headshot. Professional lighting does several things:

  • Flatters your facial features and skin tone
  • Creates dimension and depth in your portrait
  • Highlights your eyes and conveys emotion
  • Eliminates unflattering shadows or harsh lines

Unlike natural outdoor light or harsh camera flash, studio lighting is carefully controlled and positioned to complement your features. The most common professional headshot lighting setup uses key light, fill light, and back light to create a flattering, three-dimensional portrait.

2. Clear Expression and Direction

Your expression is what people will connect with most in your headshot. A great headshot captures an expression that conveys:

  • Confidence in your professional abilities
  • Approachability and warmth
  • Authenticity and genuineness
  • Appropriate emotion for your industry

Professional headshot photographers understand how to direct you to natural, confident expressions. They guide your posture, angle, and emotional tone to help you look your best.

3. Clean, Simple Composition

The strongest professional headshots use simple, uncluttered composition. Typically:

  • The background is neutral (solid color or subtle blur)
  • The framing focuses entirely on your face and shoulders
  • Distracting elements are eliminated
  • Your eyes and facial expression are the focal point

The goal is to put all attention on you—your face, your expression, and your professionalism.

4. Professional Retouching

Professional retouching enhances your headshot while keeping you looking like yourself. Good retouching:

  • Evens out skin tone
  • Minimizes blemishes and temporary imperfections
  • Enhances clarity and sharpness
  • Adjusts lighting consistency
  • Maintains natural appearance

The key is subtle enhancement—you should look like yourself, just polished and professional.

5. Proper Image Dimensions and Format

A professional headshot is delivered in formats suitable for different uses:

  • Square format (1:1) for LinkedIn and social profiles
  • Headshot format (8x10 or 4x5) for printing and submissions
  • Landscape format for websites and digital use
  • High resolution files for print quality

How to Choose a Professional Headshot Photographer

Not all photographers are skilled at headshot photography. Here's what to look for when choosing a headshot photographer:

Portfolio and Experience

Review the photographer's portfolio specifically for headshot examples. Look for:

  • Consistency in lighting quality
  • Variety in subject types (executives, actors, professionals)
  • Clear, confident expressions
  • Professional retouching
  • Years of headshot-specific experience

Understanding of Your Industry

Different industries have different headshot expectations. An executive headshot differs from an actor headshot, which differs from a corporate team photo. Choose a photographer who understands your industry and what casting directors, hiring managers, or clients expect to see.

Direction and Coaching Ability

The best headshot photographers are excellent at directing clients. They'll guide you toward natural, confident expressions and help you feel comfortable in front of the camera. Look for photographers who:

  • Make you feel at ease during the session
  • Provide clear direction without being overly technical
  • Show you proofs and get your feedback
  • Deliver consistent, professional results

Local Expertise and Studio Quality

Professional headshots require professional facilities. The photographer should have:

  • A professional studio with quality lighting equipment
  • Professional retouching workflow
  • Professional delivery and file formats
  • Local knowledge of your area and industry

What to Expect in a Professional Headshot Session

Before the Session

Before your headshot session, you'll typically:

  • Discuss your professional brand and goals
  • Get guidance on what to wear
  • Choose outfit and styling options
  • Discuss any specific needs (LinkedIn, corporate website, acting casting, etc.)

During the Session

During your session, expect:

  • Professional lighting setup (usually 30-45 minutes total time)
  • Direction and posing guidance
  • Multiple outfit or expression variations
  • Review of proofs so you can provide feedback
  • A comfortable, professional environment

After the Session

After your session, the photographer will:

  • Professional retouch your images
  • Deliver final files in multiple formats
  • Provide high-resolution images for printing
  • Supply web-ready files for LinkedIn and websites

Headshot Costs and Investment

Professional headshot photography is an investment in your career. Typical headshot costs range from $300-$1,000 depending on:

  • Photographer experience and reputation
  • Session length and number of outfit changes
  • Retouching and editing quality
  • Geographic location
  • Additional services (multiple locations, rush turnaround, etc.)

A quality headshot is worth the investment because it directly impacts how you're perceived professionally. A great headshot can lead to better LinkedIn engagement, more professional opportunities, and stronger first impressions.

Service-Specific Headshot Photography

Different professional paths require different headshot approaches:

Executive Headshots

Business leaders and executives need headshots that convey authority, professionalism, and trustworthiness. Executive headshot photography focuses on polished, confidence-conveying portraits for C-suite leaders, founders, and business owners.

Actor Headshots

Performers and actors need headshots that show range, personality, and casting potential. Actor headshot photography is specifically designed for casting directors and auditions, with emphasis on expression and character type.

Corporate Team Headshots

Organizations need consistent, professional headshots across all team members. Corporate team headshot photography ensures unified lighting, background, and color grading across your entire team.

Final Thoughts

A professional headshot is an investment in your career and professional brand. By understanding what makes a great headshot and choosing an experienced photographer, you'll have images that work as hard as you do—conveying professionalism, confidence, and approachability to everyone who sees them.

Whether you're an executive building your leadership presence, an actor pursuing casting opportunities, or a professional looking to strengthen your LinkedIn profile, a quality professional headshot is one of the best professional investments you can make.
