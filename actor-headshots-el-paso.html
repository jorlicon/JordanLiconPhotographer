The Importance of Professional Actor Headshots

Your actor headshot is your calling card at auditions. It's often the first impression casting directors have of you, and it needs to capture your personality, range, and professional presence.

A great actor headshot:

  • Shows your authentic self and personality
  • Displays clear, confident facial expression
  • Features professional lighting and composition
  • Represents your casting range and type
  • Is recent and accurately reflects your current appearance
  • Stands out in a casting pile with clarity and presence

What to Bring & Wear

Clothing: Bring 3-5 outfit options that represent your casting range. For example: casual/everyday wear, professional/business attire, dramatic/edgy looks. Simple, solid colors photograph best.

Hair & Makeup: Style your hair as you normally would. Minimal makeup is typically best for honest headshots. If you work with a makeup artist, feel free to bring them.

Attitude: Come ready to be directed and show different expressions. Most importantly, bring your personality and energy to the session.

Actor Headshot FAQ

Q: How often should I update my headshots?

A: Industry standard is every 6-12 months, or whenever your appearance changes significantly. Casting directors expect headshots that look like you at your audition.

Q: Should I have color or black & white headshots?

A: Color is standard for most actors and casting types. We recommend having 1-2 casting-friendly looks in color.

Q: Can I see proofs before purchasing additional headshots?

A: Yes. We'll show you options during the session so you can choose which looks work best for your type.

Q: Do you work with headshot submission services like Headshot Crew?

A: We provide files in all formats needed for major headshot services and casting platforms. Your headshots will work seamlessly with whichever service you choose.

Q: What if I'm not sure about my casting range?

A: We'll help you figure it out during consultation. Part of our job is understanding your type and shooting accordingly.

Q: How quickly can I get my headshots?

A: Turnaround is 3-5 business days for retouched, casting-ready images.
