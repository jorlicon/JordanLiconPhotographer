- About

El Paso commercial photographer
with strategic precision.

Jordan Licon creates commercial photography and cinematography in El Paso for clients who need images to do more than look polished. The work is built to strengthen credibility, sharpen brand perception, and give teams the kind of visual assets that sell before a conversation begins.

With a background in microbiology, biomedical research, and clinical environments, Jordan brings an unusually disciplined eye to production: precise lighting, controlled color, careful direction, and a process designed for executives, institutions, architecture firms, agencies, and commercial teams that cannot afford guesswork.

The signature is the relationship between light, optics, and scene: cinematic lighting, vintage optical character, and compositions built around how a person, place, or brand needs to be perceived. The result is imagery that feels editorial, credible, and commercially useful across websites, campaigns, proposals, press, and social platforms.

- FAQ

Common questions

What is your booking process?+
We begin with a consultation call to discuss the details of your session and answer questions. Headshot sessions start at $350 and include 1 finalized headshot and 3 outfits. Additional finalized headshots are $200 each. Once the session is scheduled, a signed contract and deposit secure your date.
Do you offer refunds or rescheduling?+
One-time rescheduling is available within 15 days of the original session date when at least 48 hours notice is provided. Without 48 hours notice, the session fee is forfeited and an additional $250 retainer may be required to reschedule. All retainer fees are nonrefundable.
What happens if I am running late to my photo shoot?+
Please call or text if you are running late. Late arrivals reduce the available shooting time, and if you are more than 30 minutes late, the session may need to be rescheduled depending on stylist availability. In that case, the standard rescheduling policy may not apply and an additional retainer may be required.
How long does a session take?+
Session length depends on the type of shoot. A typical portrait session takes about 2-3 hours, including hair and makeup styling. Executive headshot and commercial sessions may vary based on the selected tier, number of looks, locations, and production needs.
What should I wear to my session?+
Choose clothing that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Wardrobe options are discussed in more detail during the consultation call so the final choices support your role, brand, complexion, backdrop, and the intended use of the images.
Do you offer digital files for portrait, fashion, and fine art photography?+
Yes. Digital files are included with the purchase of an album or wall art product. Digital files are not available as a standalone product for portrait, fashion, and fine art sessions.
Do you offer RAW files?+
RAW files are not delivered. RAW files are unedited, unprocessed camera files intended for the photographer's workflow. The final selected images are edited and finished to represent the intended quality, color, retouching, and artistic direction of the work.
How do I view and order my images?+
A reveal and ordering session is scheduled so you can view the best images from your session and choose the ones you would like to purchase. From there, your final package, products, and selected images are designed and ordered.
Do you offer retouching services?+
Yes. Signature retouching is included on purchased images. A complimentary master retouch is also included for wall art selections to make sure enlargements are polished. Minor tattoo removal is available by request; large tattoo removal may require an additional fee because of the time involved.
What is included in a headshot session?+
Pre-shoot consultation, professional lighting, expression coaching, proof gallery, 1 finalized headshot, and 3 outfits. Additional finalized headshots are $200 each. For planning help, see the headshot preparation articles.
Do you photograph architecture and interiors?+
Yes. Architectural photography is available for architects, developers, hospitality teams, commercial real estate, and design firms. You can review the architectural portfolio.
Are you insured and FAA-certified for drone work?+
Yes. FAA Part 107 certified with commercial drone insurance for qualifying commercial and architectural projects.
